Greeting

In early 2001, 17 developers gathered near the Wasatch Mountains in Snowbird, Utah, to discuss the future of software development. The participants of this group were united by concern about the current state of affairs in the industry.

Problem - companies were paying too much attention to over-planning and documenting everything. 12 principles were united in a manifest 68 words. The essence of them is in making the processes of product creation faster by micro-planning, short production cycles and rapid response to changes.

Anything that is on the left side is more important than something on the right side.

individuals and interactions | processes and tools working product | perfect documentation customer collaboration | contract conditions responding to change | following a plan

1. In order for people to work more efficiently, processes and tools should not limit them.

In Agile they decide for themselves how to change the processes/tools of their work. To speed up the development process, people should interact directly. If they have a remote work - it is better to use a videocall (not just chats).

2. In order for customers to be satisfied, they need a working product.

“Our highest priority is to satisfy the customer through early and continuous delivery of valuable software.” Product developers should focus precisely on making sure that the product can be used as soon as possible, and not on making lists, diagrams, requirements, reports to the customer.

3. For a valuable product, omit unnecessary contract details.

Being rigidly set at the start, the details of the contract make it difficult to take into account new data and priorities that appear only during development. In order for the business value of the product to grow rapidly, the customer and the developer must communicate closely in the course of work. In this case, all changes and problems that arise are promptly handled by both parties.

4. Agile allows for iterative work and being prepared for changes at all stages to avoid delaying project risks until it is too late to make adjustments.

make work properly and in time

Plan - what?, Design - how exactly to make it, Develop - trying to make it real, Test - see how it works, Deploy - let people try it, Review - did we make what we wanted?, Yes - next feature, No - let's plan again.
Also there are more principles - making a simple project, create self-organising team, be team, but appreciate individuals, make "shots of the project" frequently
